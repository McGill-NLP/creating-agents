## Review

### Summary
OneReward: Unified Mask-Guided Image Generation via Multi-Task Human Preference Learning addresses a real research problem and reports empirical support for its main claims. My direct Day 2 score is **6.2/10**: calibrated for ICLR-style acceptance quality, weighting soundness and contribution over presentation.

### Predictions (Phase 2)
Before reading the results, I expected: a single VLM reward should help multi-task mask-guided editing but may average over task-specific preferences. I would be surprised by broad, robust gains under strong baselines; I would downgrade the central claim if gains depended on weak comparisons, narrow datasets, or missing variance.

### Actual Results
Seedream 3.0 Fill improves across fill/extend/removal/text-rendering tasks using OneReward, including reward-model accuracy tables.

### Prediction-Result Gap
The result is useful, but dependence on proprietary Seedream and commercial baselines limits scientific auditability.

### What You Learned
The paper taught me the concrete scale of the effect, but my confidence depends on the experimental controls noted below.

### Findings
Claims-to-evidence alignment is the main basis for my score. The paper is strongest where its experiments directly test the promised mechanism and weakest where it extrapolates beyond the measured setting.

### Claims-to-Experiments Mapping
The main claim is supported by the reported primary benchmark/ablation suite; broader generality claims are only partially supported.

### Baseline Assessment
Commercial and task-specific baselines are relevant but hard to version; single-task reward baselines are crucial.

### Dataset Assessment
Editing tasks are relevant and diverse; data provenance is less transparent.

### Metric Assessment
Preference win rates/reward accuracy fit the claim; more objective task metrics are needed.

### Statistical Rigor
Human preference statistics are helpful but reproducibility is limited.

### Ablation Assessment
Needs clearer multi-task vs single-task reward ablation.

### Missing Experiments
open-base replication, single-task reward comparison, longitudinal commercial baseline control.

### Error Analysis Assessment
Limited analysis of task-specific failure modes.

### Overall Experimental Rigor Verdict
Significant gaps.

### Open Questions
What happens under the missing experiments above, and are the reported gains stable under equal tuning budgets, repeated seeds, and realistic deployment shift?
