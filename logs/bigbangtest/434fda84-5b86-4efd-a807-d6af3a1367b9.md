## Review

### Summary
Erase or Hide? Suppressing Spurious Unlearning Neurons for Robust Unlearning addresses a real research problem and reports empirical support for its main claims. My direct Day 2 score is **6.8/10**: calibrated for ICLR-style acceptance quality, weighting soundness and contribution over presentation.

### Predictions (Phase 2)
Before reading the results, I expected: unlearning methods may hide rather than erase knowledge; retraining attacks should expose this. I would be surprised by broad, robust gains under strong baselines; I would downgrade the central claim if gains depended on weak comparisons, narrow datasets, or missing variance.

### Actual Results
The paper uses attribution to identify spurious inhibitory neurons and SSIUU improves robustness under adversarial and benign retraining attacks.

### Prediction-Result Gap
The failure mode is important and the evaluation is more realistic than many unlearning papers, but scale and causality remain limited.

### What You Learned
The paper taught me the concrete scale of the effect, but my confidence depends on the experimental controls noted below.

### Findings
Claims-to-evidence alignment is the main basis for my score. The paper is strongest where its experiments directly test the promised mechanism and weakest where it extrapolates beyond the measured setting.

### Claims-to-Experiments Mapping
The main claim is supported by the reported primary benchmark/ablation suite; broader generality claims are only partially supported.

### Baseline Assessment
Relevant unlearning baselines are compared; more recent jailbreak/extraction attacks would help.

### Dataset Assessment
Datasets/tasks are appropriate for LLM unlearning but not broad enough for universal claims.

### Metric Assessment
Forget/retain/relearn metrics match the claim.

### Statistical Rigor
Variance/significance is not as visible as it should be for retraining-based attacks.

### Ablation Assessment
Attribution and removal regularization ablations support the mechanism.

### Missing Experiments
larger models, longer retraining, stronger recovery attacks, user-utility analysis.

### Error Analysis Assessment
Some resurfacing analysis exists; broader failure cases are needed.

### Overall Experimental Rigor Verdict
Mostly rigorous with gaps.

### Open Questions
What happens under the missing experiments above, and are the reported gains stable under equal tuning budgets, repeated seeds, and realistic deployment shift?

### Verdict Score
6.8/10
