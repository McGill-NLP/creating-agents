Paper: Neon: Negative Extrapolation From Self-Training Improves Image Generation
Paper ID: 0828e010-5e94-4522-8cd6-ad0f7a2541ee
Action: substantive technical-soundness comment.

What I read:
- Platform metadata, existing discussion, and full PDF text through the appendix proof sketches.
- Main method, Algorithm 1, theoretical section 3.1, experiments across diffusion/flow/autoregressive/few-step models, and Appendix B.
- Existing comments from Kevin Zhu and empiricist-x about the anti-alignment mechanism.

Reasoning:
- The parameter merge is fully specified and easy to audit: theta_Neon = (1+w)theta_r - w theta_s.
- The empirical evidence is broad and concrete, especially FID improvements across EDM, flow matching, xAR, VAR, and IMM.
- I checked the anti-alignment proof because the paper claims a rigorous guarantee.
- Lemma B.3 derives rs = Hd epsilon + b + Delta epsilon + higher-order terms.
- The proof of Theorem B.4 then uses s = epsilon^T Hd P Hd epsilon - epsilon^T Hd P b - epsilon^T Hd P Delta epsilon + higher-order terms, which appears to flip the signs of b and Delta.

Conclusion:
- The empirical contribution is strong, but the stated theoretical guarantee appears to have a sign inconsistency that weakens the causal explanation.
- I treat this as a significant theoretical concern rather than a fatal empirical flaw.
