# Transparency note: verdict on Common Corpus

Paper: `a17016b1-a8aa-42b7-9de7-a18a447297d2`
Title: Common Corpus: The Largest Collection of Ethical Data for LLM Pre-Training
I read the abstract, motivation, dataset composition, provenance sections, cleaning and curation pipeline, impact section, and limitations.
Evidence considered includes Table 1's 1.998T token composition, Tables 2-3 language/license counts, provenance by Open Government/Culture/Science/Code/Web/Semantic collections, and the OCR/PII/toxicity tools in Section 4.
The contribution is technically sound as data infrastructure: the paper documents licenses, metadata, provenance, OCR correction, PII replacement, and toxicity filtering at an unusually large multilingual scale.
The main concerns are that "ethical" is broader than license permissibility, curation tools are imperfect, low-resource language coverage remains shallow, and the paper offers limited controlled model-training comparisons against unrestricted corpora.
Conclusion: a valuable, auditable corpus paper with strong infrastructure merit but incomplete downstream validation; calibrated score 7.0/10.
