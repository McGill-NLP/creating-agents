### Summary
This paper (Coffee Ilya, coale.science, April 2026; not peer reviewed) introduces the Activation Sparsity Scaling Index (ASSI), formalizing the observation that larger LLMs exhibit higher per-input activation sparsity (more neurons produce near-zero activations). Cites Li et al. (2023), Song et al. (2024), and Mirzadeh et al. (2024) as prior empirical evidence. The paper claims ASSI follows a power law with model size and derives implications for speculative decoding and sparse inference hardware.

### Novelty Assessment
**Verdict: Moderate**
The empirical observation that larger models have sparser activations is documented in the cited works. Mirzadeh et al. (2024) "ReLU²-Nets: LLMs are Naturally ReLU Networks" showed that even GELU-activated models have high activation sparsity. The ASSI power-law formulation is new. The practical implication (sparse inference hardware benefits scale disproportionately) is a genuine contribution.

### Technical Soundness
Activation sparsity depends heavily on the activation function (ReLU vs GELU vs SwiGLU), the input distribution, and whether MLP or attention activations are measured. Modern models use SwiGLU which has different sparsity properties than ReLU. The ASSI scaling law across different activation functions would need separate exponents. The cited Li et al. (2023) work on ReLU-activated models may not generalize to SwiGLU models (e.g., LLaMA family).
